# Community Shelf — UX Copy Review

**Page audited:** Landing page (`library.costico.eu`)
**Date:** March 5, 2026

---

## Overall Assessment

The existing copy is solid — it's warm, clear, and communicates the product well. The tone fits the community-oriented audience. Below are targeted refinements to sharpen clarity, reduce friction, and make every word earn its place.

**Scoring (out of 5):**
- Clarity: 4/5 — mostly clear, a few spots are vague
- Conciseness: 3.5/5 — some descriptions could be tighter
- Consistency: 3/5 — terminology shifts between sections (see notes)
- Usefulness: 4/5 — CTAs are good, some descriptions could be more benefit-driven
- Voice: 4.5/5 — warm and human, fits the audience

---

## Section-by-Section Review

### 1. Badge Pill

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current** | Free for community organizations |
| **Suggested** | Free for every community |

**Why:** "Organizations" is formal and might make informal groups (a building, a street, a friend group) wonder if they qualify. "Every community" is more inclusive and inviting.

---

### 2. Hero Headline

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current** | Your Community Library, Online |
| **Suggested** | Your Neighborhood Library, Online |

**Why:** "Neighborhood" is warmer and more specific than "community," which is already in the product name. It avoids the repetition of "Community Shelf → Community Library" and paints a clearer picture.

**Alternative:** Keep as-is if "community" is an intentional brand keyword you want to reinforce.

---

### 3. Hero Subhead

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current** | Share books, games, music, and more with your neighborhood. Community Shelf gives every community the tools to run a beautiful, modern lending library. |
| **Suggested** | Share books, games, music, and more with your neighbors. Everything you need to run a beautiful lending library — no tech skills required. |

**Why:**
- "Your neighborhood" → "your neighbors" is warmer (people, not a place)
- Cut "Community Shelf gives every community the tools to" — it's the product restating its own name. The user already knows what product they're on
- Added "no tech skills required" — addresses the #1 anxiety for community organizers
- Removed "modern" — it doesn't add concrete value (what makes it modern?)

---

### 4. Hero CTAs

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current** | `Get Started Free` / `Explore Libraries` |
| **Suggested** | `Create Your Library` / `See It in Action` |

**Why:**
- "Get Started Free" is generic SaaS language. "Create Your Library" is specific and exciting — it tells the user exactly what happens next
- "Explore Libraries" is vague. Explore what? "See It in Action" sets a clearer expectation: you'll see real examples
- Keep "Free" visible but move it to a supporting line beneath the CTA button (e.g., "Free forever for communities") rather than cramming it into the button text

---

### 5. Features Section Header

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current** | Everything You Need |
| **Suggested** | Built for Community Librarians |

**Why:** "Everything You Need" is a filler headline used on thousands of SaaS pages. "Built for Community Librarians" speaks directly to the reader's identity and makes them feel seen.

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current subhead** | A complete toolkit for running your community library — from cataloging to custom branding. |
| **Suggested subhead** | Catalog your collection, manage lending, and make your library feel like home. |

**Why:** "Complete toolkit" is abstract. The rewrite names three concrete things the user can do, which is more compelling. "Feel like home" ties to the branding feature in an emotional way.

### 6. Feature Cards

#### Catalog Management
| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current** | Organize your entire collection — books, media, games, and more — with rich metadata and cover art. |
| **Suggested** | Add books, games, music, and more — complete with cover art and details, auto-filled for you. |

**Why:** "Rich metadata" is developer-speak. Community organizers care that the information fills in automatically. Lead with the item types (concrete) rather than the abstract verb "organize."

#### ISBN Scanner
| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current** | Scan book barcodes to instantly import titles with full metadata, or bulk-import your entire collection. |
| **Suggested** | Point your phone at a barcode to add a book in seconds. Got a big collection? Bulk-import it. |

**Why:** More conversational and human. "Point your phone" is a clearer mental image than "scan barcodes." The two-sentence structure separates the two use cases cleanly.

#### Copy Tracking
| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current title** | Copy Tracking |
| **Suggested title** | Availability Tracking |

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current desc** | Track every physical copy with conditions, locations, and real-time availability status. |
| **Suggested desc** | See which items are available, who has what, and where each copy lives — all in real time. |

**Why:** "Copy Tracking" sounds like clipboard functionality to a non-librarian. "Availability Tracking" is immediately understandable. The rewrite answers the user's actual questions: "Is it available? Who has it? Where is it?"

#### Rental System
| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current title** | Rental System |
| **Suggested title** | Lending & Returns |

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current desc** | Handle borrow requests, approvals, due dates, and returns with a streamlined workflow. |
| **Suggested desc** | Members request a borrow, you approve it, they return it. Due date reminders happen automatically. |

**Why:**
- "Rental" implies payment; "Lending" is the right word for a free community library
- "Streamlined workflow" is vague SaaS jargon. The rewrite walks through the actual flow in plain language, which doubles as a mini-tutorial

#### Curated Lists
| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current** | Create themed collections and reading lists to guide your community's discovery. |
| **Suggested** | Build reading lists, seasonal picks, or "staff favorites" to help members find their next read. |

**Why:** "Guide your community's discovery" is lofty. The rewrite gives concrete examples of list types, which immediately sparks ideas for the reader.

#### Custom Branding
| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current** | Make it yours with a custom logo, color palette, and site identity that reflects your community. |
| **Suggested** | Add your logo, pick your colors, and make the library feel like it belongs to your community. |

**Why:** "Site identity" is abstract. "Make it feel like it belongs" is emotional and concrete. Minor tightening overall.

#### Your Own Space
| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current** | Each library gets its own isolated space — separate catalogs, members, and admin controls. |
| **Suggested** | Your library is completely yours — your own catalog, members, and settings, separate from everyone else. |

**Why:** "Isolated space" sounds cold/technical. "Completely yours" is warmer and conveys the same idea. "Admin controls" → "settings" is simpler.

### 7. Explore Libraries Section

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current heading** | Explore Libraries |
| **Suggested heading** | Libraries Already Using Community Shelf |

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current subhead** | Discover community libraries already powered by Community Shelf. |
| **Suggested subhead** | See what other communities have built. |

**Why:** The current heading repeats the secondary CTA button text, which is confusing when a user clicks "Explore Libraries" and lands on a section that says "Explore Libraries" again. The rewrite adds social proof (others are already using it). The subhead is tighter and more inviting.

### 8. Bottom CTA Banner

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current heading** | Start Your Library Today |
| **Suggested heading** | Ready to Share? |

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current subhead** | Create a free account and request your own community library space. We'll get you set up in no time. |
| **Suggested subhead** | Create a free account and we'll have your library up and running in minutes. |

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Current button** | Get Started Free |
| **Suggested button** | Create Your Free Library |

**Why:**
- "Start Your Library Today" is fine but generic. "Ready to Share?" ties back to the core value prop (sharing with neighbors) and feels like a natural conversation
- "Request your own community library space" has too many words and makes it sound like there's an approval process. Simplify to "we'll have your library up and running"
- "In no time" → "in minutes" is more specific and credible
- Button rewrite matches the suggested hero CTA for consistency

---

## Consistency Issues Found

1. **"Rental" vs. "Borrow/Lending"** — The feature is called "Rental System" but the description says "borrow requests." Community libraries lend, not rent. Pick one term: **lending**.

2. **"Community" overuse** — The word "community" appears 9 times on the page. Consider alternating with "neighborhood," "neighbors," "members," or "your group."

3. **"Get Started Free" appears twice** — Both hero and bottom CTA use identical text. The bottom CTA should feel like a progression, not a repeat. Differentiate them.

4. **Meta description** could be tighter:
   - **Current:** "Share books, games, music, and more with your neighborhood. Community Shelf gives every community the tools to run a beautiful, modern lending library."
   - **Suggested:** "Run a free lending library for your neighborhood. Community Shelf makes it easy to catalog, lend, and share books, games, and more."
